# Nationalism as Revolution's Fuel

> "The fact that both the French Revolution and the Russian Revolution turned into nationalist movements seems to indicate that in modern times nationalism is the most copious and durable source of mass enthusiasm."

The ideas that start revolutions — economic justice, social reform, human rights — rarely sustain them. Abstract causes produce intellectual commitment but not the visceral fervor needed to hold a mass movement together through hardship, violence, and internal dissent. Nationalism provides what abstractions cannot: identity.

The pattern is consistent. Movements framed around solving concrete problems — poverty, corruption, inequality — generate less mass enthusiasm than movements framed around national or ethnic identity. A call to fix infrastructure does not move people the way a call to reclaim a homeland does. Nationalist movements carry a fervor, an exaltation, and a sense of possibility that policy-oriented movements rarely match — because they are not asking people to think. They are asking people to belong.

**Nationalism attaches a cause to identity rather than ideas.** Ideas require thought, debate, and tolerance for nuance. Identity requires only membership. As [[freedom-movements-and-lost-liberty]] establishes, belonging is what the masses are actually seeking from movements — not the intellectual content of the cause. Nationalism offers a ready-made identity that feels ancient and eternal, even when it was manufactured recently.

This is why political movements so frequently turn nationalist regardless of their starting point. The original goals — land reform, workers' rights, democratic governance — get subordinated to the question of who *we* are and who *they* are. The movement stops being about what it wants to build and becomes about what identity it represents. Once that shift happens, anyone outside the identity becomes a natural enemy, which is why [[hatred-as-unifying-agent]] functions so effectively within nationalist frameworks.

The relationship to [[secularism-vs-ethno-nationalism]] is one of direct tension. Nationalism as a political tool is extraordinarily effective. But the ethno-nationalist state it tends to produce is structurally exclusionary — capable of generating pride only in its majority, while reducing everyone else to permanent outsiders.
