# Mass Migration as Pressure Valve and Detonator

Emigration and mass movements solve the same psychological problem. Both offer escape from a self that has become unbearable — one through geography, the other through ideology. A frustrated person who crosses a border gets the same relief as a frustrated person who dissolves into a cause: the old, intolerable self is left behind.

This makes emigration a **pressure valve**. When migration is available, some of the energy that would otherwise fuel political movements gets redirected. The most restless leave. The regime survives not because it addressed any grievances but because the people most likely to act on those grievances are no longer in the country.

But emigration is also a **detonator**. When frustrated people leave en masse, they find each other abroad — same grievances, now concentrated in communities with more resources, more freedom to organise, and less to lose. The frustration does not dissolve with distance. It crystallises. Scattered domestic discontent becomes a focused diaspora movement, often more radical than anything the homeland produces.

This pattern appears consistently across history. Diaspora communities tend to be more politically radical than the populations they left behind. They carry the frustration without the moderating contact with the reality on the ground. The homeland evolves — conditions change, compromises are made, new problems replace old ones. The diaspora's political imagination freezes at the moment of departure and hardens over time. The grievance is preserved in the form it had when they left, untouched by the complexities that might have softened it.

The connection to [[why-the-abjectly-poor-never-revolt]] is instructive. The abjectly poor cannot emigrate any more than they can revolt — both require a minimum threshold of resources, hope, and imagination. It is the layer just above destitution — people with enough to leave but not enough to be satisfied — that feeds both movements and migrations. When those people leave, they do not stop being frustrated. They find a new place to be frustrated together.
